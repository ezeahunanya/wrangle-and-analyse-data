Act Report¶

Introduction¶

This document aims to explain the visualisation and insights produced in the wrangle_act.ipynb document.

Analysis¶

Insight 1¶

A summary of statistical measures of the cleaned dataframe can be seen by calling the describe method on the dataframe. The data shows that on average the first prediction has the highest confidence level followed by the second and third, with a mean level of 0.605, 0.138 and 0.061 respectively. Interestingly the spread of the data is highest for the first prediction and decreases in the second and third predictions, with values of 0.266, 0.102 and 0.052 respectively. Fig 1. shows a histogram of the distribution of confidence levels for each image prediction which visually represents the results shown with the describe method. The first prediction is the best one.

Insight 2¶

Using the value_counts method the most common dog breed in the archive is the Labrador Retriever. This is confirmed by all 3 predictions with 135, 95 and 75 occurrences in the first, second and third predictions.

Insight 3¶

The most common names are Cooper, Charlie and Lucy with an occurrence of 10 times. They have an average retweet count of 1689, 2369 and 2944 and an average favourite count of 6472, 9440 and 9074 respectively.

Insight 4¶

If a good dog is defined as one with a rating of 11 or 12 out 10 then 46.8% of dogs in the dataframe are good dogs. This result is would be very underwhelming to Brent who hypothesised every dog was rated 11s and 12s.
